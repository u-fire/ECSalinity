13 # Introduction
14 The EC-Salinity I2C device can measure electrical conductivity in the range of
15 0.5 - 62mS and salinity from 2 - 42.
16 <hr>
17 
18 # Characteristics
19 - **EC range**: 0.5 - 62mS
20 - **Salinity range**: 2 - 42
21 - **Temperature range**: -2 - 35C
22 - **Interface**: I2C
23 - **Current use**: 10mA peak, low idle current ~1mA
24 - **Voltage range**: 2.7 - 5.5V
25 
26 It uses the standard Arduino Wire library to interface with the device.
27 It's operating principle is based on sending a very short-duration DC pulse
28 from one probe pin and measuring the conductivity on the other pin. The conductivity
29 is then converted to a temperature compensated Siemen. Salinity is also derived
30 from the measure and is in PSU, PPT, and PPM.
31 <hr>
32 
33 # Connections
34 Making the connections to the device is as follows:
35 | EC Salinity Probe | Master device |
36 | :---------------: | :---------------: |
37 | GND | GND |
38 | SCL | SCL |
39 | SDA | SDA |
40 | VCC | 3 - 5V |
41 
42 The temperature probe should be connected as follows:
43 | EC Salinity Probe | Temperature Probe |
44 | :---------------: | :---------------: |
45 | + | VCC |
46 | D | Data |
47 | - | GND |
48 
49 The EC probe has two pins and can be connected either way.

52 # Setup
53 When the device is powered for the first time, it will be uncalibrated and must
54 be calibrated to provide accurate readings. The calibration setting
55 of the probe is referred to as the cell constant and represented by
56 #EC_Salinity::K. An uncalibrated device will show K = -1. To begin the calibration process,
57 place the thermometer and probe in a calibration solution then call the
58 #EC_Salinity::calibrateProbe function.
59 
60 For best results, the probe should be cleaned with distilled water and then placed
61 in the solution for 5-10 minutes before the probe is used. Note that any
62 turbidity, air bubbles, large particles, etc will effect readings. An unstable
63 temperature will decrease accuracy as well.
64 
65 When calibrating the probe, it is important to consider the expected temperature
66 range. EC measurements are very temperature dependent, effecting the results by
67 approximately 2% per degree C. The probe should be calibrated at the median
68 expected temperature.
69 
70 Likewise, the probe should be calibrated in the median expected EC range. For example,
71 if you are planning to measure the salinity of an aquarium, you might expect
72 readings Â±5 from 35PPT (equivalent to 53mS). So a 53mS solution for calibration
73 would be appropriate, whereas a hydroponic calibration might be 2.77mS.
74 
75 Another consideration is the placement of the probe. When the probe sends out a
76 pulse of electricity, it leaves the probe in 360 degrees. If it is near a metal
77 surface, you will experience fringing effects. Generally, if the probe is calibrated
78 where it is to be used, fringing effects are not a concern, but if you calibrate
79 the probe in, for example, a small cup of plastic solution, then place it in a solution that
80 has a large amount of interference in the form of metal or electrical activity,
81 the measurements will vary widely.
82 
83 #### Dual Point Calibration
84 Once the probe itself is calibrated, you can optionally use dual point calibration
85 for more accurate results between two predesignated points. Two calibration solutions
86 are required for this calibration, the low and high values you expect to measure between.
87 
88 1. Determine the lowest and highest measurement you expect in mS. For example, to
89 measure salinity in an aquarium, the lowest level you would expect to measaure might
90 be 30 PPT and the highest might be 38 PPT. These points are referred to as
91 referenceLow and referenceHigh
92 
93 2. Using the calibrated probe and a calibration solution at referenceLow, call
94 #EC_Salinity::measureEC and see what the actual reading is. Do the same for
95 referenceHigh. These readings are referred to as readingLow and readingHigh.
96 As an example, the readingLow might be 27 and readingHigh might be 40.
97 
98 3. Call #EC_Salinity::setDualPoint
99 ~~~~
100  ec.setDualPoint(30, 38, 27, 40);
101 ~~~~
102 
103 4. Each call to #EC_Salinity::measureEC will use the provided numbers to adjust
104 the reading. It can be disabled by
105 ~~~~
106  ec.useDualPointCalibration = false;
107 ~~~~

110 # Probe Selection
111 A basic, but adequate, probe is included with the device. It will work reliably
112 and accurately within the specifications listed above, however it is not specifically
113 designed to be an EC probe. A professional probe can cost up to several hundred
114 dollars. If you need the advantages of such an accurate device, this hobby-level
115 project is not appropriate for you. However, most people don't need to measure
116 EC to within 0.1uS reliably every time, in fact, a relatively wide range in
117 accuracy will still be quite acceptable for many use-cases.
118 
119 Any 2-electrode probe can be attached to the device. Keep in mind
120 that metal, water, and electricity don't typically cooperate. Things that effect the lifespan
121 include the chemicals it is exposed to, if it is occasionally exposed to air,
122 the number of measurements taken, and the types of metal used.
123 Deposit buildup will effect readings as well as chemical reactions that take
124 place on the probe surface. It is very likely that it will require regular
125 maintenance in the form of cleaning or recalibration.

127 # Use
128 Once the probe has been calibrated, a reading can be taken. The probe should
129 be placed in the solution to be measured and #EC_Salinity::measureEC called to
130 start a measurement. For best results, the probe should be cleaned with distilled
131 water and left in the solution for 5 - 10 minutes. Be sure no air bubbles or debris
132 are on the probe points.
133 
134 After the measurement is taken, the following class variables are updated:

149 #### Temperature Compensation
150 If the solution to be measured is seawater, be sure to
151 use the temperature compensation coefficient #EC_Salinity::tempCoefSalinity,
152 otherwise use #EC_Salinity::tempCoefEC as the parameter. If another compensation
153 value is desired, it can be provided as a regular float value.
154 
155 Temperature compensation can be disabled by setting #EC_Salinity::useTempCompensation
156 to false.
157 Readings can also be adjusted to a single temperature. In many modes of use,
158 it is commonly adjusted to 25C. This can be done with the following:
159 
160 ~~~~
161  ec.tempCompensationConstant = 25;
162 ~~~~
163 At initialization, it is set to -1 and the actual temperature is used. To re-enable
164 compensation, set it back to -1.
165 
166 #### Accuracy
167 The accuracy of the device can be adjusted by modifying #EC_Salinity::accuracy.
168 By default, it is set to what is considered high accuracy (EC_HIGH_ACCURACY). Nine measurements are
169 taken. A running median sort is applied and the middle third of the nine
170 measurements are averaged together to provide the final result. Any number of
171 measurements can be taken as long as it is evenly divisible by 3. The least number of
172 measures possible is 3, as defined by EC_LOW_ACCURACY.
173 
174 ~~~~
175 ec.accuracy = 15;
176 ~~~~
177 
178 #### Measurement Time
179 Each measurement takes approximately 1 second. A majority of the time is spent
180 waiting for the temperature measurement to finish. The EC portion of the measurement
181 is done extremely quickly to avoid electrolysis effects.

184 # Notes on Measuring Seawater
185 Salinity can be accurately measured between a range of 2 - 42, between the -2 - 35 C.
186 -1 is returned if it is not within the above limitations.
